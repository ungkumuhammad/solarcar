## 3. Toyota Challenger Class — Technical Specifications

### 3.1 Solar Array
| Parameter | 2027 Limit |
|---|---|
| Maximum solar collector area | **6 m²** |
| Allowed cell technologies | Crystalline silicon, thin-film, multi-junction (concentrator restricted) |
| Array orientation | Fixed to vehicle body; no tracking mechanisms |

### 3.2 Energy Storage (Battery)
| Parameter | 2027 Limit |
|---|---|
| Maximum on-board stored energy | **3 kWh (10.8 MJ)** |
| Allowed chemistries | Lithium-ion, LiFePO₄ (others with approval) |
| Battery condition at race start | May be fully charged |
| Battery replacement | Not permitted during race; replacement due to breakdown incurs time penalty |

### 3.3 Vehicle Dimensions & Weight
| Parameter | Limit |
|---|---|
| Maximum length | 5.8 m |
| Maximum width | 2.3 m |
| Maximum height | Not specified (subject to official doc) |
| Ground clearance | Must clear 75 mm speed humps |
| Minimum wheels | 3 |
| Occupancy | Single driver; driver swap permitted at control stops |

### 3.4 Safety
- Roll bar or equivalent structure required
- Seat belt / harness required
- Fire extinguisher on board
- Rear-view mirrors or cameras required
- Brakes on all wheels
- Headlights, tail lights, indicators required (for public road compliance)
- EMF emissions compliance required

## 4. Race Operations

### 4.1 Driving Hours
| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Earliest start | 08:00 daily (08:30 on Day 1) |
| Latest finish | 17:00 daily |
| Maximum driving window | 9 hours/day (8.5 hrs Day 1) |
| Extension | Up to 10 minutes past 17:00 permitted; compensated by equivalent delayed start next morning |
| Night driving | Prohibited |

### 4.2 Speed Limits
| Territory | Road speed limit |
|---|---|
| Northern Territory | 130 km/h |
| South Australia | 110 km/h |

> Note: The NT–SA border is at Kulgera/Marla (~1,670 km from Darwin). Speed limit drops to 110 km/h from there to Adelaide.

### 4.3 Control Stops
- **9 control stops** between Darwin and Adelaide
- **Mandatory 30-minute stop** at each control point
- During the stop: array must be tilted for maximum solar charging; driver must exit vehicle and press buzzer to start the 30-minute timer
- Limited maintenance only during stops — no structural repairs
- Each stop has a **window** by which the car must arrive; missing the window results in penalties or exclusion

### 4.4 Overnight Stops
| Location | Approximate cumulative km |
|---|---|
| Day 1 stop | ~800 km from Darwin (near Tennant Creek) |
| Day 2 stop | ~1,600 km from Darwin (near Alice Springs area) |
| Day 3 stop | ~2,300 km from Darwin (near Coober Pedy area) |
| Day 4 stop | ~2,800 km from Darwin (near Port Augusta area) |

- Vehicles must stop at the designated overnight camp; no overnight driving
- Charging via solar array is permitted during overnight stops (no grid charging)

### 4.5 Overtaking & Road Rules
- All Australian road rules apply
- Vehicles must use public roads and obey all traffic laws
- Support convoy of up to 4 vehicles permitted per team

## 5. Scrutineering
- Technical inspection (scrutineering) takes place before the race start in Darwin
- All vehicles must pass scrutineering to be eligible to start
- Key checks: solar array area measurement, battery energy measurement, safety equipment, dimensions

## 6. Penalties (Summary)
| Infraction | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Arriving late at control stop window | Time penalty or exclusion |
| Exceeding battery energy limit | Disqualification |
| Operating outside driving hours | Demerit points / time penalty |
| Exceeding speed limits | Standard road penalties + event demerit |
| Battery replacement (breakdown) | Time penalty |

## 7. Changes from 2025 → 2027 (Challenger Class)

| Parameter | 2025 | 2027 |
|---|---|---|
| Solar area | 6 m² | 6 m² (unchanged) |
| Battery energy | 11 MJ (~3.06 kWh) | ~3 kWh (effectively unchanged) |
| Race dates | August 24–31 | August 22–29 |

> The 2025 event was the first held in the Southern Hemisphere winter (August), which reduced solar irradiance compared to the traditional October schedule. The 2027 event continues in August.
